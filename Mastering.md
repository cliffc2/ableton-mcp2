Recommended Master Chain OrderConsole Emulation / Channel Strip (optional but great for glue)Adds analog console character, subtle saturation, and cohesion right at the start.
Examples: Waves SSL, API, Neve emulations, or Ableton’s own stock tools if you want to keep it light.

Corrective / Subtractive EQ (Cleaning)High-pass filter (remove rumble below 20–30 Hz).
Cut problem frequencies (mud, harshness).
This cleans the signal before further processing.

Compression / Glue Compressor (often multiband or bus-style)Gentle glue (2–4 dB gain reduction, slow attack, fast-medium release).
Controls dynamics and adds punch/cohesion.

Saturation / Tape Emulation (ATR)Tape (ATR-102 style, J37, etc.) or harmonic saturation.
Adds warmth, harmonics, and smooth compression-like behavior.
Many put tape here — it sounds natural after dynamics are tamed but before final tone shaping.

Pro Tips for Heavy Bass in D&BLayer your bass (sub + mid bass + top) on separate tracks or groups.

Automate filter cutoff or drive for movement in drops/breaks.
Always A/B with your kick — they must sit perfectly together.
